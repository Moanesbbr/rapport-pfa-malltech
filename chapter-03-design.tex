\chapter{System Design and Architecture}

\section*{Introduction}
This chapter translates the requirements into a concrete system design, covering
the global architecture, database schema, API structure, and key interaction flows.

\section{Global Architecture}

\subsection{Three-Service Architecture}

Malltech is decomposed into three independently deployable TypeScript services:

\begin{longtable}{|m{4cm}|m{2.5cm}|m{7cm}|}
    \hline
    \textbf{Service} & \textbf{Framework} & \textbf{Role} \\
    \hline
    \endhead
    \endfoot
    \endlastfoot
    \hline
    \texttt{malltech-backend} & NestJS 11 & RESTful API, business logic, WebSocket gateway, MongoDB \\
    \hline
    \texttt{malltech-frontend} & Next.js 16 & Customer storefront (SSR/SSG) \\
    \hline
    \texttt{malltech-admin} & Next.js 16 & Administration backoffice dashboard \\
    \hline
    \caption{Malltech service decomposition}
    \label{tab:services}
\end{longtable}

\subsection{Deployment Architecture}

\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\fbox{\parbox{0.85\textwidth}{\centering\vspace{2cm}
\textit{[Deployment Architecture Diagram]}\\
\small Internet $\rightarrow$ OVH VPS $\rightarrow$ Caddy (reverse proxy, TLS)\\
Caddy $\rightarrow$ \{malltech-frontend, malltech-admin, malltech-backend\}\\
malltech-backend $\leftrightarrow$ MongoDB\\
Netdata (monitoring all containers)\\
GitHub Actions (CI/CD pipelines)
\vspace{2cm}}}
\caption{Malltech deployment architecture}
\label{fig:deployment}
\end{figure}

All containers share a \texttt{malltech} Docker bridge network. Only Caddy
exposes ports 80 and 443 to the host; all other services use internal Docker
networking exclusively.

\subsection{Order Data Model}

The order schema captures the complete commercial transaction:

\begin{longtable}{|m{3cm}|m{2.5cm}|m{8cm}|}
    \hline
    \textbf{Field} & \textbf{Type} & \textbf{Description} \\
    \hline
    \endhead
    \endfoot
    \endlastfoot

    \hline
    \texttt{total} & Number & subtotal + shippingFee - discount \\
    \hline

\section{API Design}

\subsection{REST API Structure}

The API follows REST conventions with resource-based URL routing:

\begin{longtable}{|m{5cm}|m{2cm}|m{6.5cm}|}
    \hline
    \textbf{Endpoint} & \textbf{Method} & \textbf{Description} \\
    \hline
    \endhead
    \endfoot
    \endlastfoot
    \hline
    \texttt{/auth/login} & POST & Authenticate and receive JWT \\
    \hline
    \texttt{/auth/register} & POST & Create a customer account \\
    \hline
    \texttt{/products} & GET & List products (filterable, paginated) \\
    \hline
    \texttt{/products/:slug} & GET & Get single product by slug \\
    \hline
    \texttt{/basket} & GET/POST/PUT/DELETE & Basket CRUD \\
    \hline
    \texttt{/orders} & POST & Place an order \\
    \hline
    \texttt{/orders/my} & GET & Customer order history \\
    \hline
    \texttt{/admin/orders} & GET & Admin — all orders \\
    \hline
    \texttt{/admin/orders/:id/status} & PATCH & Admin — update order status \\
    \hline
    \texttt{/admin/invoices/:id/pdf} & GET & Admin — download invoice PDF \\
    \hline
    \caption{Selected API endpoints}
    \label{tab:api_endpoints}
\end{longtable}
